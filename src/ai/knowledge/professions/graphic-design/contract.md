# Graphic Design Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating a simple service agreement from an approved graphic design project. This guide supplements the contract generation module and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Logo design
- Brand identity
- Social media graphics
- Posters
- Brochures
- Packaging design
- Presentation design
- Illustration
- Marketing materials

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Required sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Payment Terms
6. Revision Policy
7. Intellectual Property
8. Acceptance
9. Termination
10. Signatures

---

## 3. Section Guidance

### Project Scope

- Summarize the agreed design services.
- Reflect the approved proposal.
- Specify the agreed design category.
- Do not introduce additional creative work.

### Deliverables

List only agreed deliverables, for example:

- Logo
- Brand identity package
- Brand guidelines
- Social media graphics
- Posters
- Brochures
- Packaging artwork
- Presentation slides
- Illustrations
- Source files
- Print-ready files

Exclude deliverables not included in the approved proposal.

### Timeline

- Use agreed milestones or delivery schedule.
- Leave unspecified dates as placeholders.

### Payment Terms

- Use the agreed pricing and payment schedule.
- Do not invent penalties or legal obligations.

### Revision Policy

- State the agreed number or scope of revision rounds.
- If unspecified, leave as a placeholder.

### Intellectual Property

- State ownership according to the agreed arrangement.
- Distinguish between working files and final deliverables only when explicitly agreed.

### Acceptance

Acceptance may be based on approval of the final design deliverables.

Use neutral wording.

### Termination

Include a simple project cancellation section.

Do not generate complex legal remedies.

---

## 4. Conditional Sections

Include only when applicable:

- Brand guideline delivery
- Source file delivery
- Print production support
- Multiple design concepts
- Commercial usage rights
- Confidentiality

---

## 5. Section Selection Rules

- Always include required sections.
- Include conditional sections only when supported by the approved proposal or deal data.
- Do not generate obligations that are not part of the agreed engagement.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders when information is unavailable, for example:

- Client name
- Brand or project name
- Delivery dates
- Payment amounts
- Revision rounds
- Commercial usage scope
- Signing date

Do not fabricate missing information.

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate:

- Legal advice
- Trademark registration advice
- Copyright registration advice
- Marketing strategy agreements
- Software development responsibilities
- Printing supplier agreements
- Employment agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Any custom legal provisions requiring professional review
