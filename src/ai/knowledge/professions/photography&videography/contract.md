# Photography & Videography Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating simple photography and videography service agreements.

This guide supplements the common contract generation logic and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Portrait photography
- Wedding photography
- Event photography
- Product photography
- Food photography
- Corporate photography
- Real estate photography
- Commercial videography
- Social media content production

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Generate contracts using the following sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Description
3. Deliverables
4. Schedule
5. Payment Terms
6. Client Responsibilities
7. Revision Policy
8. Usage Rights
9. Cancellation
10. Acceptance

---

## 3. Clause Guidance

### Project Description

Describe:

- project type
- purpose
- location
- estimated duration

Example:

> Photography coverage for a one-day corporate event at the client's office.

---

### Deliverables

Specify only agreed deliverables.

Examples:

- Edited photographs
- Raw photographs
- Highlight video
- Full-length video
- Short-form social media clips

Avoid promising unspecified deliverables.

---

### Schedule

Include:

- shooting date(s)
- editing period
- delivery date

If exact dates are unavailable, use milestone-based wording.

---

### Payment

Include:

- total fee
- deposit (if applicable)
- remaining balance
- payment schedule

Do not generate penalty clauses.

---

### Client Responsibilities

Examples include:

- provide venue access
- provide event schedule
- obtain required permissions
- coordinate participants
- provide branding materials when required

Only include responsibilities relevant to the project.

---

### Revision Policy

Specify:

- number of revision rounds
- scope of revisions
- additional revision fees (if applicable)

Do not promise unlimited revisions unless requested.

---

### Usage Rights

Clarify ownership and permitted use of deliverables.

Common approaches include:

- client receives commercial usage rights
- freelancer retains portfolio rights
- copyright transfers only upon full payment if specified

Do not infer legal ownership arrangements beyond the agreed scope.

---

### Cancellation

Include:

- cancellation before project date
- cancellation during production
- handling of deposits
- payment for completed work

Keep terms simple and project-specific.

---

## 4. Conditional Clauses

Include only when applicable.

| Situation | Optional Clause |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Outdoor shoot | Weather contingency |
| Multi-day production | Multi-session schedule |
| Travel required | Travel expenses |
| Drone footage | Drone operation responsibility |
| Commercial campaign | Model or location release responsibilities |
| Urgent editing | Expedited delivery |

---

## 5. Clause Selection Rules

Include clauses only when supported by the accepted proposal or project information.

Do not introduce:

- legal warranties
- indemnification
- liability limitations
- jurisdiction clauses
- statutory legal provisions

When information is missing, use neutral placeholders instead of assumptions.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders for information not yet agreed.

Typical placeholders include:

- Client Name
- Project Title
- Shooting Date
- Location
- Deliverables
- Delivery Date
- Total Fee
- Deposit
- Final Payment Date

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate clauses relating to:

- employment agreements
- talent or employment contracts
- licensing beyond the agreed project
- legal copyright transfer documentation
- trademark agreements
- litigation or dispute resolution
- tax obligations
- regulatory compliance

Such requests should be identified as requiring professional legal review.
