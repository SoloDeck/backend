# Photography & Videography Proposal Guide

## 1. Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating photography and videography proposals from qualified leads. This guide supplements the proposal generation framework and does not define proposal formatting.

---

## 2. Supported Project Types

Generate proposals for engagements including:

- Portrait photography
- Wedding photography
- Event coverage
- Product photography
- Food photography
- Corporate photography
- Real estate photography
- Commercial videography
- Social media content production

---

## 3. Proposal Structure

A photography or videography proposal should generally include:

1. Project summary
2. Coverage scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Pricing
6. Assumptions
7. Exclusions

---

## 4. Scope Interpretation

### Converting Client Requirements into Scope

Convert qualified intake information into production scope.

| Intake Information | Proposal Scope |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Project type | Primary photography or videography service |
| Purpose | Intended business or personal outcome |
| Location | Shooting environment |
| Required deliverables | Final media outputs |
| Estimated duration | Coverage duration |

---

### Handling Missing Information

If important information is unavailable:

- State it as a clarification item.
- Do not invent shooting requirements or production details.
- Limit the proposal to confirmed project scope.

---

### Handling Assumptions

Distinguish between:

- Confirmed requirements
- Reasonable production assumptions
- Recommendations requiring client approval

Do not present assumptions as confirmed project requirements.

---

## 5. Deliverable Guidance

### Typical Deliverables

Depending on project scope, deliverables may include:

- Edited photographs
- RAW image files
- Highlight video
- Full-length video
- Short-form social media videos
- Color-corrected media
- Digital image gallery
- Optimized social media exports

---

### Deliverable Wording

Describe deliverables as completed media assets.

Prefer:

- Edited product photo collection
- Wedding highlight video
- Corporate event gallery
- Short-form promotional videos

Avoid:

- Take photos
- Record videos
- Shoot event

---

### Optional Deliverables

Only include when supported by the inquiry:

- Drone photography
- Additional photographers
- Additional videographers
- Same-day previews
- Rush delivery
- Album design
- Extended editing
- Multiple export formats

---

## 6. Timeline Guidance

### Common Project Phases

Typical phases include:

- Pre-production planning
- On-site photography or filming
- Post-production editing
- Client review
- Final delivery

---

### Timeline Estimation Guidance

Estimate timelines according to:

- Coverage duration
- Project complexity
- Number of deliverables
- Editing workload

Avoid precise schedules when project scope is incomplete.

---

### Dependency Considerations

Identify dependencies including:

- Event schedule
- Location access
- Weather conditions
- Client availability
- Venue restrictions
- Content approval

---

## 7. Pricing Guidance

### Pricing Factors

Consider factors such as:

- Project type
- Coverage duration
- Shooting location
- Number of photographers or videographers
- Equipment requirements
- Editing effort
- Deliverables requested

---

### What Affects Estimates

Estimated pricing may increase due to:

- Multiple shooting locations
- Extended coverage
- Extensive post-production
- Travel requirements
- Drone operation
- Rush delivery

---

### When Not to Estimate

Avoid estimating when:

- Coverage duration is unclear.
- Shooting location is unknown.
- Deliverables are undefined.
- Production requirements remain uncertain.

Recommend clarification instead.

---

## 8. Common Assumptions

### Safe Assumptions

Unless stated otherwise, proposals may reasonably assume:

- Digital delivery
- Standard color correction
- Professional editing
- Industry-standard equipment

---

### Assumptions Requiring Confirmation

Do not assume:

- Number of final edited photos
- Video duration
- Travel expenses
- Drone usage
- Additional crew
- Delivery deadline
- Usage or licensing rights

These should be confirmed with the client.

---

## 9. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not include services outside photography and videography unless explicitly requested and supported.

Examples include:

- Graphic design
- Website development
- Digital marketing
- Copywriting
- Event planning
- Printing services

When mixed-domain requests are identified, generate the proposal only for the photography or videography portion and identify remaining requests separately.
