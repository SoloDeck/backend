# Digital Marketing Consulting Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating a simple service agreement from an approved digital marketing consulting project. This guide supplements the contract generation module and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Marketing strategy
- Digital advertising
- SEO consulting
- Content planning
- Analytics and reporting
- Campaign management
- Social media marketing
- Lead generation campaigns

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Required sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Payment Terms
6. Revision Policy
7. Intellectual Property
8. Acceptance
9. Termination
10. Signatures

---

## 3. Section Guidance

### Project Scope

- Summarize the agreed marketing services.
- Reflect the approved proposal.
- Specify the agreed marketing objectives and channels.
- Do not introduce additional consulting services.

### Deliverables

List only agreed deliverables, for example:

- Marketing strategy
- Campaign setup
- Advertising campaigns
- SEO recommendations
- Content calendar
- Performance reports
- Analytics dashboard
- Campaign optimization reports

Exclude deliverables not included in the approved proposal.

### Timeline

- Use agreed campaign duration, milestones, or reporting schedule.
- Leave unspecified dates as placeholders.

### Payment Terms

- Use the agreed pricing and payment schedule.
- Do not invent penalties or legal obligations.

### Revision Policy

- State the agreed revision or optimization scope.
- If unspecified, leave as a placeholder.

### Intellectual Property

- State ownership according to the agreed arrangement.
- Do not infer ownership of advertising accounts, creative assets, or analytics data.

### Acceptance

Acceptance may be based on delivery of agreed consulting services or reporting milestones.

Do not guarantee marketing outcomes or business performance.

### Termination

Include a simple project cancellation section.

Do not generate complex legal remedies.

---

## 4. Conditional Sections

Include only when applicable:

- Advertising account management
- Campaign monitoring
- Monthly reporting
- Ongoing optimization
- Training or consultation sessions
- Confidentiality

---

## 5. Section Selection Rules

- Always include required sections.
- Include conditional sections only when supported by the approved proposal or deal data.
- Do not generate obligations that are not part of the agreed engagement.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders when information is unavailable, for example:

- Client name
- Campaign name
- Marketing channels
- Campaign duration
- Reporting frequency
- Payment amounts
- Signing date

Do not fabricate missing information.

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate:

- Legal advice
- Financial guarantees
- Guaranteed marketing results
- Advertising platform policies
- Business consulting outside the agreed scope
- Employment agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Any custom legal provisions requiring professional review
