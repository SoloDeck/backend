# Digital Marketing Proposal Guide

## 1. Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating digital marketing proposals from qualified leads. This guide supplements the proposal generation framework and does not define proposal formatting.

---

## 2. Supported Project Types

Generate proposals for engagements including:

- Marketing strategy
- Paid advertising
- Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
- Content planning
- Campaign management
- Analytics and reporting
- Social media marketing
- Email marketing

---

## 3. Proposal Structure

A digital marketing proposal should generally include:

1. Project summary
2. Marketing objectives
3. Scope of services
4. Deliverables
5. Timeline
6. Pricing
7. Assumptions
8. Exclusions

---

## 4. Scope Interpretation

### Converting Client Requirements into Scope

Convert qualified intake information into marketing scope.

| Intake Information | Proposal Scope |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Marketing objective | Primary business objective |
| Marketing channels | Campaign platforms |
| Target audience | Audience definition |
| Services required | Scope of services |
| Current marketing status | Starting point for engagement |

---

### Handling Missing Information

If important information is unavailable:

- State it as a clarification item.
- Do not invent campaign goals, budgets, or target audiences.
- Limit the proposal to confirmed project scope.

---

### Handling Assumptions

Distinguish between:

- Confirmed requirements
- Reasonable marketing assumptions
- Strategic recommendations

Do not present assumptions as confirmed project requirements.

---

## 5. Deliverable Guidance

### Typical Deliverables

Depending on project scope, deliverables may include:

- Marketing strategy
- Campaign setup
- Advertising campaigns
- SEO recommendations
- Content calendar
- Audience research
- Performance reports
- Analytics dashboard
- Optimization recommendations

---

### Deliverable Wording

Describe deliverables as measurable marketing outputs.

Prefer:

- Facebook advertising campaign
- SEO audit report
- Monthly performance report
- Content calendar

Avoid:

- Do marketing
- Run ads
- Improve SEO

---

### Optional Deliverables

Only include when supported by the inquiry:

- Competitor analysis
- Keyword research
- Creative recommendations
- A/B testing
- Monthly strategy meetings
- Ongoing optimization

---

## 6. Timeline Guidance

### Common Project Phases

Typical phases include:

- Discovery
- Strategy development
- Campaign setup
- Campaign execution
- Monitoring
- Optimization
- Performance reporting

---

### Timeline Estimation Guidance

Estimate timelines according to:

- Number of marketing channels
- Campaign complexity
- Services requested
- Reporting frequency

Avoid precise schedules when campaign scope is incomplete.

---

### Dependency Considerations

Identify dependencies including:

- Client approval
- Advertising account access
- Existing marketing assets
- Budget approval
- Content availability

---

## 7. Pricing Guidance

### Pricing Factors

Consider factors such as:

- Number of marketing channels
- Services provided
- Campaign complexity
- Campaign duration
- Reporting requirements
- Management effort

---

### What Affects Estimates

Estimated pricing may increase due to:

- Multi-channel campaigns
- Large advertising budgets
- Frequent optimization
- Extensive reporting
- Advanced analytics
- Ongoing consulting

---

### When Not to Estimate

Avoid estimating when:

- Marketing objectives are unclear.
- Campaign scope is undefined.
- Advertising budget is unknown.
- Required services remain uncertain.

Recommend clarification instead.

---

## 8. Common Assumptions

### Safe Assumptions

Unless stated otherwise, proposals may reasonably assume:

- Digital delivery of reports
- Industry-standard marketing tools
- Periodic performance reporting
- Client approval before campaign launch

---

### Assumptions Requiring Confirmation

Do not assume:

- Advertising budget
- Campaign duration
- Target KPIs
- Existing brand assets
- Platform access
- Reporting frequency
- Success guarantees

These should be confirmed with the client.

---

## 9. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not include services outside digital marketing unless explicitly requested and supported.

Examples include:

- Website development
- UI/UX design
- Graphic design
- Copywriting beyond agreed campaign content
- Photography
- Legal or business consulting

When mixed-domain requests are identified, generate the proposal only for the digital marketing portion and identify remaining requests separately.
