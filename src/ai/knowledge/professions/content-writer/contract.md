# Content Writing Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating a simple service agreement from an approved content writing project. This guide supplements the contract generation module and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Website copy
- Blog articles
- Product descriptions
- Email copy
- Advertisements
- Social media content
- Scripts
- Press releases

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Required sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Payment Terms
6. Revision Policy
7. Intellectual Property
8. Acceptance
9. Termination
10. Signatures

---

## 3. Section Guidance

### Project Scope

- Summarize the agreed writing services.
- Reflect the approved proposal.
- Specify the agreed content type and objective.
- Do not introduce additional writing services.

### Deliverables

List only agreed deliverables, for example:

- Website copy
- Blog articles
- Product descriptions
- Email campaigns
- Advertisement copy
- Social media posts
- Video scripts
- Press releases

Include agreed content quantity where available (articles, pages, or word count).

Exclude deliverables not included in the approved proposal.

### Timeline

- Use the agreed delivery schedule or milestones.
- Leave unspecified dates as placeholders.

### Payment Terms

- Use the agreed pricing and payment schedule.
- Do not invent penalties or legal obligations.

### Revision Policy

- State the agreed number or scope of revision rounds.
- If unspecified, leave as a placeholder.

### Intellectual Property

- State ownership according to the agreed arrangement.
- Do not infer copyright transfer or exclusive usage rights.

### Acceptance

Acceptance may be based on delivery of the agreed content and completion of the agreed revision process.

Use neutral wording.

### Termination

Include a simple project cancellation section.

Do not generate complex legal remedies.

---

## 4. Conditional Sections

Include only when applicable:

- SEO optimization
- Keyword research
- Content strategy
- AI-assisted writing disclosure
- Ongoing content production
- Confidentiality

---

## 5. Section Selection Rules

- Always include required sections.
- Include conditional sections only when supported by the approved proposal or deal data.
- Do not generate obligations that are not part of the agreed engagement.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders when information is unavailable, for example:

- Client name
- Project name
- Content topics
- Delivery dates
- Payment amounts
- Revision rounds
- Word count or content quantity
- Signing date

Do not fabricate missing information.

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate:

- Legal advice
- Copyright registration advice
- Trademark advice
- Publishing agreements
- Marketing performance guarantees
- Employment agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Any custom legal provisions requiring professional review
