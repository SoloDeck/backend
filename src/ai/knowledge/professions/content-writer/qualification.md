# Content Writing Qualification Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific interpretation rules for qualifying content writing inquiries. This guide supplements the Lead Qualification Framework and does not define scoring.

---

## 1. Domain Scope

### Supported Engagements

- Website copy
- Blog articles
- Product descriptions
- Email copy
- Advertising copy
- Social media content
- Video scripts
- Press releases
- SEO content
- Landing page copy

### Out-of-Scope Engagements

- Graphic design
- UI/UX design
- Software development
- Digital marketing strategy
- Photography and videography
- Legal or technical document drafting requiring specialist expertise

---

## 2. Intake Field Interpretation

### Content Type

Treat the selected content type as the primary deliverable.

| Content Type | Typical Deliverable |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Website Copy | Website pages |
| Blog | Articles |
| Product Descriptions | Product catalog content |
| Email | Email campaign copy |
| Advertisement | Promotional copy |
| Social Media | Social media posts |
| Script | Video or audio script |
| Press Release | Press announcement |

If the inquiry conflicts with the selected content type, flag the inconsistency.

---

### Content Objective

Treat the selected objective as the primary communication goal.

| Objective | Typical Focus |
|-----------|---------------|
| Inform | Educational or informational content |
| Sell | Sales and conversion copy |
| Educate | Instructional content |
| Promote | Marketing communication |
| Entertain | Audience engagement |

Multiple unrelated objectives may indicate multiple content pieces.

---

### Target Audience

Use the target audience to determine writing style and messaging.

Examples:

- Customers
- Business owners
- Students
- Professionals
- General public

Missing target audience reduces confidence in content direction.

---

### Tone of Writing

Treat the selected tone as the expected communication style.

| Tone | Typical Style |
|------|---------------|
| Professional | Business communication |
| Friendly | Conversational |
| Formal | Official communication |
| Casual | Informal writing |
| Persuasive | Conversion-focused copy |

Conflicts between the selected tone and the inquiry should be flagged for clarification.

---

### Approximate Content Volume

Treat content volume as the primary indicator of project size.

Examples:

- Number of articles
- Word count
- Number of pages

Large or undefined content volumes may require clarification before estimation.

---

## 3. Qualification Rubric

### Strong Qualification Evidence

- Content type identified
- Content objective identified
- Target audience identified
- Writing tone identified
- Content volume identified

---

### Requires Clarification

- Generic request ("need content")
- Unclear communication objective
- Missing target audience
- Undefined writing tone
- Undefined content volume
- Conflicting structured fields and inquiry

---

### Scope Consistency

Evaluate whether the structured fields describe a single coherent writing engagement.

| Situation | Interpretation |
|-----------|----------------|
| Blog + Educate + Professional + 5 articles | Consistent |
| Product Descriptions + Sell + Friendly | Consistent |
| Website Copy + Blog + Press Release + Script | Possible multiple engagements |
| Casual tone selected but inquiry requests formal corporate communication | Inconsistent |

---

### Feasibility Signals

Identify, but do not reject:

- Large content volume with minimal budget
- Unrealistic delivery timeline
- Multiple unrelated content types
- Insufficient information for estimating writing effort

---

## 4. Mixed-Domain Guidance

Separate content writing work from requests belonging to other professions.

| Request | Qualification |
|---------|---------------|
| Write website copy | Content Writing |
| Design website layout | Out of Scope |
| Build the website | Out of Scope |
| Manage advertising campaigns | Out of Scope |

Qualify only the content writing portion and flag remaining requests for manual assessment.
