# Content Writing Proposal Guide

## 1. Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating content writing proposals from qualified leads. This guide supplements the proposal generation framework and does not define proposal formatting.

---

## 2. Supported Project Types

Generate proposals for engagements including:

- Website copy
- Blog articles
- Product descriptions
- Email campaigns
- Advertisements
- Social media content
- Video scripts
- Press releases

---

## 3. Proposal Structure

A content writing proposal should generally include:

1. Project summary
2. Content scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Pricing
6. Assumptions
7. Exclusions

---

## 4. Scope Interpretation

### Converting Client Requirements into Scope

Convert qualified intake information into writing scope.

| Intake Information | Proposal Scope |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Content type | Primary writing deliverable |
| Content objective | Writing purpose |
| Target audience | Intended readers |
| Tone of writing | Writing style |
| Content volume | Workload estimate |

---

### Handling Missing Information

If important information is unavailable:

- State it as a clarification item.
- Do not invent topics, messaging, or publication channels.
- Limit the proposal to confirmed project scope.

---

### Handling Assumptions

Distinguish between:

- Confirmed requirements
- Reasonable editorial assumptions
- Recommendations requiring client approval

Do not present assumptions as confirmed project requirements.

---

## 5. Deliverable Guidance

### Typical Deliverables

Depending on project scope, deliverables may include:

- Website copy
- Blog articles
- Product descriptions
- Marketing emails
- Advertisement copy
- Social media captions
- Video scripts
- Press releases

---

### Deliverable Wording

Describe deliverables as completed content assets.

Prefer:

- Five SEO blog articles
- Product description set
- Website homepage copy
- Email marketing sequence

Avoid:

- Write blogs
- Make content
- Write some copy

---

### Optional Deliverables

Only include when supported by the inquiry:

- Keyword optimization
- Content revisions
- Meta titles and descriptions
- Content outline
- Content strategy recommendations
- Formatting for publication

---

## 6. Timeline Guidance

### Common Project Phases

Typical phases include:

- Requirements confirmation
- Research
- Draft writing
- Client review
- Revisions
- Final delivery

---

### Timeline Estimation Guidance

Estimate timelines according to:

- Content volume
- Research requirements
- Content complexity
- Revision rounds

Avoid precise schedules when project scope is incomplete.

---

### Dependency Considerations

Identify dependencies including:

- Client brief
- Reference materials
- Brand guidelines
- Review feedback
- Subject matter expertise

---

## 7. Pricing Guidance

### Pricing Factors

Consider factors such as:

- Content type
- Word count
- Number of deliverables
- Research effort
- Technical complexity
- Revision requirements

---

### What Affects Estimates

Estimated pricing may increase due to:

- Technical or specialized topics
- Extensive research
- SEO optimization
- Large content volume
- Multiple revision rounds

---

### When Not to Estimate

Avoid estimating when:

- Content scope is unclear.
- Content volume is undefined.
- Research requirements are unknown.
- Deliverables remain uncertain.

Recommend clarification instead.

---

## 8. Common Assumptions

### Safe Assumptions

Unless stated otherwise, proposals may reasonably assume:

- Digital document delivery
- Standard proofreading
- Original content
- Industry-standard writing practices

---

### Assumptions Requiring Confirmation

Do not assume:

- Exact word count
- Number of revisions
- SEO requirements
- Keyword research
- Publication responsibility
- Brand voice guidelines
- Reference materials

These should be confirmed with the client.

---

## 9. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not include services outside content writing unless explicitly requested and supported.

Examples include:

- Graphic design
- Website development
- Marketing campaign management
- Photography
- Video production
- Translation
- Legal or technical consulting

When mixed-domain requests are identified, generate the proposal only for the content writing portion and identify remaining requests separately.
