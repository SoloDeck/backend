# UI/UX Design Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating a simple service agreement from an approved UI/UX design project. This guide supplements the contract generation module and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Mobile application design
- Website design
- Dashboard design
- Desktop application design
- Design system creation
- Product redesign
- UX improvement projects
- Wireframing and prototyping

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Required sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Payment Terms
6. Revision Policy
7. Intellectual Property
8. Acceptance
9. Termination
10. Signatures

---

## 3. Section Guidance

### Project Scope

- Summarize the agreed design services.
- Reflect the approved proposal.
- Specify whether the engagement covers UX research, UI design, redesign, or design systems.
- Do not introduce additional design work.

### Deliverables

List only agreed deliverables, for example:

- User flows
- Wireframes
- High-fidelity UI designs
- Interactive prototype
- Design system
- UX research report
- Developer handoff assets

Exclude deliverables not included in the approved proposal.

### Timeline

- Use agreed project phases or milestones.
- Leave unspecified dates as placeholders.

### Payment Terms

- Use the agreed pricing and payment schedule.
- Do not invent penalties or legal obligations.

### Revision Policy

- State the agreed number or scope of revision rounds.
- If unspecified, leave as a placeholder.

### Intellectual Property

- State ownership according to the agreed arrangement.
- Do not infer ownership of design assets beyond the agreement.

### Acceptance

Acceptance may be based on completion of agreed design deliverables, review cycles, or final design approval.

Use neutral wording.

### Termination

Include a simple project cancellation section.

Do not generate complex legal remedies.

---

## 4. Conditional Sections

Include only when applicable:

- UX research participation
- User testing
- Design system maintenance
- Developer handoff support
- Post-delivery consultation
- Confidentiality

---

## 5. Section Selection Rules

- Always include required sections.
- Include conditional sections only when supported by the approved proposal or deal data.
- Do not generate obligations that are not part of the agreed engagement.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders when information is unavailable, for example:

- Client name
- Project name
- Delivery dates
- Payment amounts
- Revision rounds
- Approval period
- Signing date

Do not fabricate missing information.

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate:

- Legal advice
- Regulatory compliance clauses
- Software implementation responsibilities
- Software maintenance agreements
- Marketing service agreements
- Employment agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Any custom legal provisions requiring professional review
