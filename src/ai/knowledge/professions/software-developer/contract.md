# Software Development Contract Guide

## Purpose

Defines profession-specific guidance for generating a simple service agreement from an approved software development deal. This guide supplements the contract generation module and does not provide legal advice.

---

## 1. Supported Project Types

- Websites
- Web applications
- Mobile applications
- SaaS platforms
- Internal systems
- APIs
- Backend services
- Automation systems
- System integrations

---

## 2. Contract Structure

Required sections:

1. Parties
2. Project Scope
3. Deliverables
4. Timeline
5. Payment Terms
6. Revision Policy
7. Intellectual Property
8. Acceptance
9. Termination
10. Signatures

---

## 3. Section Guidance

### Project Scope

- Summarize the agreed services.
- Reflect the approved proposal.
- Do not introduce new work.

### Deliverables

- List agreed deliverables.
- Use concrete deliverable names.
- Exclude work not included in scope.

### Timeline

- Use agreed milestones or delivery schedule.
- Leave unspecified dates as placeholders.

### Payment Terms

- Use agreed pricing and payment schedule.
- Do not invent penalties or legal obligations.

### Revision Policy

- Describe the agreed revision allowance.
- If unspecified, leave as a placeholder.

### Intellectual Property

- State ownership according to the agreed arrangement.
- Do not infer ownership terms.

### Acceptance

- Describe how deliverables are considered accepted.
- Use neutral wording.

### Termination

- Include a simple project cancellation section.
- Do not generate complex legal remedies.

---

## 4. Conditional Sections

Include only when applicable:

- Maintenance
- Deployment
- Hosting
- Technical Support
- Warranty
- Confidentiality

---

## 5. Section Selection Rules

- Always include required sections.
- Include conditional sections only when supported by the approved proposal or deal data.
- Do not generate sections for services that are not part of the agreement.

---

## 6. Placeholder Guidance

Leave placeholders when information is unavailable, for example:

- Client name
- Project address
- Delivery dates
- Payment amounts
- Revision count
- Signing date

Do not fabricate missing information.

---

## 7. Out-of-Scope Requests

Do not generate:

- Legal advice
- Regulatory compliance clauses
- Tax advice
- Employment agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Shareholder agreements
- Software licensing beyond the agreed project
- Any custom legal provisions requiring professional review
